Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 May 2026

Savvey is a UK snap-to-reorder app run by Savvey Ltd. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with UK GDPR. This policy explains exactly what data passes through our systems, why, who else sees it, and what you can do about it.

What we collect

When you use Savvey, the following data is processed:

  • Product searches — the text you type, photos you snap (typically 3 frames stitched into one AI request), barcodes you scan, or URLs you paste. We use these to identify the product and return price information. Tracking parameters (utm_*, gclid, fbclid, etc) are automatically stripped from URLs before they reach our AI.
  • Anonymised usage analytics via PostHog — events such as "snap captured," "result shown," "retailer chip tapped." These contain no personal identifiers, no IP retention, no cross-app tracking.
  • Local browser storage — recent searches, your preferences (e.g. swipe-hint dismissed, GDPR consent banner dismissed). This stays on your device. We never access it from our servers.
  • Cached responses — for performance, we cache product identifications by content hash for up to 24 hours in our key-value store (Vercel KV). The cache key is a hash of your input; nothing in the cache is linked to you.

What we do not collect

  • We do NOT collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any other personal identifiers.
  • We do NOT have user accounts. There is no sign-up, login, or password.
  • We do NOT collect financial information, payment details, or purchase history.
  • We do NOT track your activity outside of Savvey.
  • We do NOT sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing.
  • We do NOT retain photos you snap beyond the duration of the AI identification request (typically a few seconds).
  • We do NOT use your data to train AI models. Our AI provider (Anthropic) does not retain content sent through their API.

How we use your data

  • Anthropic Claude (AI): product searches and snapped photos are sent to Anthropic Claude Haiku for identification. Anthropic processes these on our behalf under their Data Processing Agreement and does not retain content beyond the immediate request lifecycle. See anthropic.com/privacy for Anthropic's policy.
  • SerpAPI: verified Amazon UK prices and Google Shopping retailer deep-links are fetched via SerpAPI on your behalf. SerpAPI does not retain queries beyond their own logging requirements.
  • Open Food Facts: for barcode scans, we may pre-resolve the EAN via the Open Food Facts public database to improve identification accuracy on grocery and household items. No personal data is sent.
  • Skimlinks: when you click a non-Amazon retailer link, the click may be routed through Skimlinks (a UK affiliate aggregator). Skimlinks measures clicks and conversions for affiliate commission tracking. Skimlinks may set first-party cookies on the destination retailer's domain (not on Savvey). See skimlinks.com/privacy-policy.
  • Anonymous analytics via PostHog tell us which features users engage with so we can improve them. No PII, no cross-app tracking.

Safety filtering

To protect users and our service, Savvey includes a server-side filter that detects when a snapped image or query appears to be of something other than a product (for example, a person, a sensitive object, or content that doesn't fit the app's purpose). On a match, the app shows a friendly redirect message ("That doesn't look like a product. Try snapping the packaging") and discards the input without storing it. We log only the fact that a filter event occurred, not the content that triggered it.

Where your data is processed

  • Vercel (hosting + serverless functions): US/EU edge network
  • Anthropic Claude: US (under EU–US Data Privacy Framework)
  • SerpAPI: US
  • PostHog (analytics): EU (Frankfurt)
  • Skimlinks (affiliate routing): UK / US
  • Open Food Facts: France (public open-data API)

Retention

We retain:

  • Cached responses: up to 24 hours, then automatically deleted by TTL.
  • Anonymous analytics events: 12 months in PostHog, then deleted.
  • Server logs (technical errors only, no content): 30 days.
  • Your photos and queries: not retained beyond the immediate request lifecycle.

Your rights under UK GDPR

As a UK user, you have the right to:

  • Access the data we hold about you
  • Request rectification or deletion of your data
  • Object to processing
  • Restrict processing
  • Data portability
  • File a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk)

Because we don't collect personally identifying information and don't have user accounts, we typically have no data tied to you specifically. If you want to clear your local browser storage, append ?fresh=1 to the Savvey URL or use your browser's site-data controls.

Affiliate links + revenue

Savvey may earn a small commission when you buy via the Amazon UK link or other retailer links shown in the app (Currys, John Lewis, Argos, Boots, Tesco and others, routed via Skimlinks). This is how we keep the app free. The price you pay never changes — it's the same as if you'd typed the URL yourself. We disclose this clearly on every result screen.

AI use

Savvey uses Anthropic's Claude Haiku 4.5 to identify products from photos and to provide UK retail context (typical price bands, popular alternatives, product tier classification — Premium / Mid-tier / Budget). The AI's role is identification and context only. Every price you see on a result screen is verified live from a real retailer listing — no AI-generated prices are ever shown to users. AI can make mistakes — always verify product details on the retailer's page before purchasing. No images or queries are used to train any AI model.

Children

Savvey is not designed for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has used Savvey, contact us and we'll remove any traces.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be flagged in the app and at the top of this page.
